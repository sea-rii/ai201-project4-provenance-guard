Appeals vs Disagreements

Signal disagreement rate measures the percentage of submissions where one signal contradicted the other two. Persistent disagreement (above ~20%) is an early warning that signal weights or thresholds need recalibration — the ensemble is less confident than its combined score suggests. Appeal rate is a lagging indicator of false positives: creators who know their work is human-written and push back. Track both together to distinguish systematic miscalibration from edge-case noise.
